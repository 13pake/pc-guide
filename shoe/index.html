Shoe

LSZ "Shoe"

  • Good setup as it uses S's and Z's in the setup - solve chance increase
  • Closing boxes using JS, LSZ, IL, TT, TST/TZT
  • Very flexible: Can extend into Anti-pco, Hill, Tub

4p Setups

Shoe is similar to Jaws in that it only takes 3 pieces to build. Since most setups often consist of 4 pieces to take full advantage of see-7, you will often add another piece on the side opposite of Shoe.

Extensions

3p Shoe offers incredible flexibility in converting to different shapes.


Box

Shoe has a lot of flexibility when coverting to a Box setup. The S piece is almost always placed on top of the other S piece.

Box shapes split the solve into two boxes, and this is the most common way to utilize Shoe. The size of the Box can be 5x4, 6x4, 7x4, or even 8x4. Typically only requires 1 L/J to close.

Tub

Most commonly seen on 2nd, this shape is useful if you have another T + S/Z coming and 2 LJ. Tub is also common on 4th and 6th.

Anti-PCO

If you can't box off the shoe, most likely you will have to convert the Shoe into an Anti-PCO. Anti-PCO is a very versatile shape. Can be often extended into Box, Tub, or PCO. Most of the time Anti-PCO will need a 2nd T or I skim to solve.

Solving Methods

General solving technique

When solving shoe, you will typically try to split the solve into two sections. The shoe side, and the other side.

The first thing to look for is how you are going to fill out the shoe shape. There are several options. JS is the obvious choice, and IL can also close off the 5x4.

Skimming

Typically you will either skim the 2nd row or 3rd row when solving. Be careful to fill out the “tip” of the shoe when skimming the 2nd row.
